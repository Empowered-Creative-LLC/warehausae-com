intro_prose: >-
  Since 1880, York Hospital has served the York community and has grown to become the regional’s leader in advanced specialty care. A multi-million-dollar expansion is underway, pushing the largest
  hospital in York County to become one of the top 10 largest hospitals in Pennsylvania. The project plans include an eight-story surgical and critical care tower, new women’s floor, ICU and operating
  rooms, all with state-of-the-art medical technology.
narrative:
  we_listen: >-
    The project is expected to take five years to complete, with two older buildings on the campus being demolished to accommodate the expansion. Additional parking spaces will be added to the campus
    to provide convenient access for patients and visitors.
  we_design: >-
    Getting the project off to a successful start required detailed land planning and survey work. Had the project been in a green field, the design would have been straightforward. However, with an
    in-fill project located on an already fully developed campus with steep topography, the intricacies of implementing the client’s desired design were much more complex.
  we_deliver: >-
    Warehaus’ Civil team played a key role in coordination between subconsultants, two municipalities, the client and the design team. Subconsultants for the project include site survey, utility
    survey, geotechnical, environmental, and traffic related services. The design team includes Cannon Design, providing architectural and structural engineering services, and WSP, providing MEP
    engineering. Additionally, Warehaus prepared multiple concept plans and provided the following additional services: Zoning Variances, Land Development, NPDES Permitting, Construction
    Administration, and Post Construction Services.
