intro_prose: >-
  The Kaltreider-Benfer Library, part of York County Library System, unveiled its newly expanded and renovated space in 2023. This marks the culmination of a transformative journey. The
  Kaltreider-Benfer Library project is the crowning achievement of the 'Imagine. Discover. Connect.' capital campaign involving dedicated volunteers, local support, matching grants, and financial
  assistance from the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. This project is the last of the campaign’s renovations, which also included Kreutz Creek Library and Martin Library.
narrative:
  we_listen: >-
    The library staff orchestrated the relocation of a staggering 45,000 books, shelves, and furniture to pave the way for construction. During this transition, the library continued to serve its
    patrons from a temporary site.


    To prepare for construction, Warehaus’ civil engineering team performed work associated with a stormwater management plan that collected runoff from the existing and new roofs. They also provided
    recommendations for site improvements where curb and pavement were failing, and they worked to coordinate new utility connections.
  we_design: >-
    The $2 million renovation and construction initiative resulted in an impressive 3,676 sf expansion. This elevated the York County library’s footprint to a total of 13,358 sf. The library’s design
    prioritizes transparency, emphasizing outward views and public spaces. The children’s area promotes hands-on learning and sensory experiences. The main browsing area offers diverse study spaces,
    reading nooks, and meeting spots. A dedicated teen area with collaborative workstations and seating is also included. Open spaces offer better visual quality from the main desk throughout the
    library. Mechanical systems and restrooms were also upgraded. These enhancements signify the library’s enduring commitment to community service.
  we_deliver: >-
    Robert Lambert, President of York County Library System remarked, “I think the community will embrace the three updated and reimagined libraries,” says Lambert. “It is with great pride that we
    unveil our third campaign project, Kaltreider-Benfer Library. The newly expanded and renovated library features dedicated children and teen spaces, an open service area, additional computers,
    seating, and meeting spaces.” Kaltreider-Benfer Library re-opened to the public in May 2023.
