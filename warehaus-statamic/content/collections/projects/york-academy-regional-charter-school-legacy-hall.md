lead_heading: Adaptive Reuse for Education
intro_prose: >-
  When York Academy Regional Charter School (YARCS) needed to expand its Upper School to meet the demands of a growing student population, they turned to Warehaus to help bring their vision to life.
  Working alongside Wagman Construction, Warehaus transformed a neighboring, long-abandoned industrial building into Legacy Hall—a vibrant educational space that blends the building’s historic
  character with York Academy’s forward-thinking mission. The project added seven new classrooms and supporting spaces within a narrow footprint, requiring creativity and precision to maximize every
  square foot.
narrative:
  we_listen: >-
    The aggressive timeline was the project’s greatest challenge. Portions of the construction occurred while school was in session, with all work needing to be complete before the start of the
    2025–2026 school year. Warehaus designed a flexible layout that streamlined plumbing and utilities, thoughtfully integrated exposed HVAC systems, and used a mix of old and new finishes to balance
    cost, efficiency, and character. Legacy Hall’s proximity to the Warehaus office allowed for frequent, on-site collaboration with Wagman and city officials, enabling rapid decision-making and
    seamless communication throughout the process.
  we_design: >-
    As with many adaptive reuse projects, unexpected conditions arose. When the second-floor framing was uncovered, the team discovered it had been built on old wood pallets resting atop a brick
    barrel-vaulted arch system. Warehaus quickly worked with the structural engineer and Wagman to design a code-compliant, cost-effective solution that also provided sound absorption for the
    classrooms. Later, unforeseen conflicts with underground gas and electrical lines required extensive coordination with the city and water authority to reroute utilities safely and efficiently.
  we_deliver: >-
    Warehaus’ history with YARCS—which includes architectural and civil design services for both their renovated Lower School and newly constructed Upper School—laid the groundwork for this project’s
    success. Building on this trusted partnership, Legacy Hall was delivered on schedule, opening its doors before the start of the 2025 school year. Today, the former industrial space stands as a
    bridge between past and future, providing a dynamic, inspiring environment where York Academy students can grow, learn, and thrive.
