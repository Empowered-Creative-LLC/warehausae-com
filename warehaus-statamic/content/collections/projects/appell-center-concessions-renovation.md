intro_prose: >-
  Nestled in downtown York, PA, the Appell Center stands as a hallmark of architectural grandeur, playing host to two historic theaters – The Capitol Theatre (1906) and the Strand Theatre (1925). The
  iconic landmark is set to undergo a transformation to its concessions area, continuing a design partnership with Warehaus that began in 2019. Driven by the need to enhance theater flow and patron
  experience, the Appell Center re-engaged Warehaus in 2022 to lead the design and construction phases for its three concessions areas. The project’s unique challenge is to modernize these beloved
  spaces while preserving their rich heritage.
narrative:
  we_listen: >-
    Warehaus acknowledges the significance of preserving the Appell Center’s historical legacy while creating spaces that resonate with contemporary audiences. Beyond revitalizing concessions spaces,
    the project has encompassed a comprehensive approach to meet the needs of the modern theatergoer. The integration of updated design and optimized food service facilities aims to seamlessly blend
    aesthetics with functionality.
  we_design: >-
    The project’s program centers on revitalizing the three interior concession stands. However, the scope expanded to address additional areas of the building based on requirements for Redevelopment
    Assistance Capital Program (RACP) funding. Warehaus offered comprehensive support in navigating the RACP funding landscape, ensuring the inclusion of essential roofing replacements and providing
    bid assistance. The project scope also encompassed the design of fourteen exterior door replacements, with MEP upgrades facilitated by Gatter & Diehl and food service design handled by Clark Food
    Services. As the project continued to take shape, Warehaus remained steadfast in its commitment to seamlessly harmonize historical significance with contemporary functionality, further solidifying
    the Appell Center’s role as a cultural cornerstone.
  we_deliver: >-
    The impact of the revitalized concessions areas and overarching building improvements reverberate far beyond aesthetics. The Appell Center’s ability to cater to theatergoers in York and its
    neighboring regions is bolstered, aligning with the evolving landscape of technology and food service innovations.
