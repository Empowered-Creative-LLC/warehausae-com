narrative:
  we_listen: >-
    To design the 603,000 sf distribution facility, Warehaus utilized 30+ years’ experience with distribution and logistics-intensive facilities. A design/build delivery system was key to meeting the
    aggressive schedule.
  we_design: >-
    Warehaus significantly reduced the amount of time needed for construction and minimized the impact of winter conditions by designing the structure with a system of 193 precast concrete panels and
    a structural steel frame. The roofing frame system includes steel joists and girders with metal roof decking painted white on the interior surface. The roof is a 60 mil adhered EPDM roofing
    system. The warehouse floor is a 7-inch, fiber-reinforced concrete slab with Glazit, a non-toxic cement additive for waterproofing and sealant for concrete.
  we_deliver: >-
    By working closely with a material handling designer, they incorporated efficiencies throughout the building shell, for maximum storage capacity. Through the design/build partnership, architects,
    engineers and construction estimators worked together to concurrently fast-track production. The entire facility was constructed in just 6 months – one month ahead of the originally scheduled
    completion date.
