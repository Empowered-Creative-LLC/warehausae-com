intro_prose: >-
  Kinsley Properties and Royal Square Development & Construction have joined forces for the adaptive reuse and conversation of a vacant office building into a 43-unit apartment complex. This 38,000 sf
  project, located at 204 N. George Street, marks the revival of the former Fox Baking Company. The building’s history as a bakery and its subsequent transition to an office setting culminated in four
  years of vacancy, prompting this ambitious conversion.
narrative:
  we_listen: >-
    This trend of repurposing former office spaces into contemporary apartments is gaining momentum, with the Fox Bakery project being the latest addition to downtown York’s urban revitalization.
    Financed by York-based Traditions Bank, the project harnesses state and federal tax credits for historic preservation.
  we_design: >-
    Warehaus leads the architectural design services for this conversion, which includes not only 43 apartment units but also two ground-floor retail spaces, a mail area, bike storage, and a community
    gym. The property, owned by Kinsley since the 1990s, boasts a prime location with dual aspects – one entrance facing North George Street and the main entrance accessible from West Gay Avenue.
  we_deliver: Royal Square’s integration of these apartments into the city’s fabric undoubtedly enriches the Downtown York residential landscape, elevating the urban living experience for residents.
